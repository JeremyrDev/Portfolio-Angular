    Etsy Shop Pros and Cons

    Alright, so WAY back in the day my girlfriend and I both sold candles on Etsy. Maybe not that long ago, but due to COVID and other reasons, we've put it on hold for a bit. I'm a web developer by trade and I've started working pretty exclusively with Etsy shop owners who were fed up with the limitations of Etsy's platform; along with it's long list of other issues.

    Here's a quick ist of pros and cons I've gathered working with other shop owners:
    Etsy Pros:
    • It's easy to get started and setup a shop
    • It has decent functionality for shop owners
    • Handles Transactions and other complicated things
    • Built in analytics
    • Very popular platform, big potential traffic
    Etsy Cons:
    • FEES, the fees are horrible. 5% plus 3% + $0.25 per transaction...
    • No Email Lists...
    • Provides little "Exposure" on their platform
    • ZERO branding and customization
    • Advertising is usually pretty ineffective
    • You can only sell handmade items
    • I already said fees, but the listing fees every 3 months
    • Huge competition, markets are usually super saturated
    • Expensive shipping labels
    • Sometimes confusing to use overall
    • Not as professional as an established, branded website

    So what can you do?

    Pretty unanimously, Etsy shop owners talk about the most success coming from having an etsy shop in conjuction with their own separate website. Having both is often best.

    Having your own website/online shop is very freeing! No $0.20 listing fees, no 5% + 3% + $0.25 transaction fees, no over paying for shipping labels, and no in-effective Etsy advertising. With your own website all you pay for is hosting which is very minimal. You have full capacity to style and brand your website to make it look however you want to make your shop much more professional. Your orders, customer management, analytics, and product management is completely streamlined and allows for A LOT of saved time and hair pulling.

    That's something I can help with. I've gotten to work with shop owners and I used to be one myself so I understand transitioning your products over and managing all of it. Every person I've worked with has had their sales increase and their day to day lives optimized greatly. Let me help you get the same for your shop!
